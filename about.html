Meet the author:

The Master of Mythology

Rick Riordan, often referred to as “The Master of Mythology,” is a renowned author whose storytelling has inspired generations of readers. Born on June 5, 1964, in San Antonio, Texas, Riordan developed a deep love for storytelling at a young age. His journey to becoming a beloved author wasn’t a straightforward one. Initially, Riordan pursued a career in education, teaching middle school English and history. It was during this time that he discovered his passion for mythology, a subject he eagerly shared with his students. This love for ancient tales and his experience teaching young people ultimately led him to write books that blend mythological themes with contemporary settings. His warm and relatable personality, combined with his talent for humor and adventure, quickly connected him with readers worldwide.

What sets Riordan apart from other authors is his ability to create stories that are not only entertaining but also deeply meaningful. Despite his global success, Riordan remains grounded in his values of inclusivity, diversity, and educational empowerment. His work is shaped by his personal experiences, including his role as a father, his time as a teacher, and his belief in the power of stories to change lives. Throughout his career, Riordan has been an advocate for neurodiversity and has worked tirelessly to create characters that reflect the real-world challenges young people face. His dedication to connecting with his readers on a personal level has made him a much-loved figure in literature and a true mentor for the next generation of readers and writers.

Early Life:
  • Born on June 5, 1964, in San Antonio, Texas, Rick developed an early interest in Greek mythology.
  • Graduated from the University of Texas at Austin with a degree in English and History, laying the foundation for his writing.
  • Was actively involved in creative writing during college, which nurtured his passion for storytelling.
  • Worked as a middle school teacher, teaching English and History, where he shared his love for mythology with students.
  • His son, Haley, diagnosed with ADHD and dyslexia, inspired the creation of Percy Jackson, a hero with similar learning differences.
